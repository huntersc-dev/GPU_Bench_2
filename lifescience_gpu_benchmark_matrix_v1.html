Platform Verdicts
▌ NVIDIA — Hopper & Blackwell
H200 SXM5
141GB HBM3e · 4.8TB/s · 67 TFLOPS FP64 · NVLink 4
GxP21 CFR Pt 11HIPAA
  • Proven regulatory baseline — validated in GxP, 21 CFR Part 11, HIPAA environments
  • Optimal for existing FP64 Cryo-EM and structural biology HPC pipelines
  • Most cost-effective for pure GROMACS / AMBER molecular dynamics batch workloads
  • Low integration risk for existing CUDA-based bioinformatics stacks
Verdict: Right-size for institutions not yet scaling to 70B+ clinical LLM inference. Stable, auditable platform for MD simulation and genomics processing.
B100 SXM
192GB HBM3e · 8TB/s · 90 TFLOPS FP64 · NVLink 5
GxP21 CFR Pt 11EMA
  • 1.7× H200 memory bandwidth — strong uplift for GROMACS multi-system MD and Cryo-EM
  • NVLink 5 doubles peer bandwidth vs H200 — better for multi-GPU protein folding
  • Natural H100/H200 cluster refresh — no CUDA code changes for existing pipelines
Verdict: Ideal evolutionary upgrade for pharma HPC on H100/H200. Best TCO for mixed MD simulation + imaging inference without full Blackwell capex.
B200 SXM
192GB HBM3e · 8TB/s · 112 TFLOPS FP64 · 2.25 PF BF16 · FP8
GxP21 CFR Pt 11FDA SaMD
  • Highest single-GPU FP64 in standard Blackwell — HPC sweet spot for molecular simulation
  • FP8 Transformer Engine enables 70B BioMedLM / clinical NLP at production throughput
  • Structured sparsity for accelerating genomic classification and imaging AI models
Verdict: The 2025-2026 single-GPU sweet spot — balances HPC FP64 depth and clinical AI inference breadth without NVL72 rack commitment.
B300 SXM
288GB HBM3e · 16TB/s · ~140 TFLOPS FP64 est. · ~2.8 PF BF16 est.
GxPEMAICH E6
  • 288GB VRAM + 16TB/s — eliminates GROMACS memory bottleneck for large biomolecular systems
  • Estimated best single-GPU for STREAM-bound genomic data processing pipelines
  • ~2× B200 on memory bandwidth — full Parabricks WGS pipeline fits in-GPU without spill
Verdict: Best single-server option for institutions needing GB300 memory without full NVL72 capex. Pre-GA — estimates only.
GB200 NVL72
192GB · 8TB/s/GPU · 2.5 PF BF16 (rack) · NVLink 5 · 130TB/s fabric
GxPFDA SaMDEMA
  • NVL72 rack as single GPU abstraction — full protein language model and clinical LLM in-rack

  • Transformative for multi-omics AI — consolidate genomics, imaging, and NLP onto one fabric
Verdict: Pharma / large hospital system inflection point. Justify capex by consolidating MD simulation, imaging AI, and regulatory NLP onto one fabric.
GB300 NVL72

▌ AMD — Instinct MI Series
MI300X
192GB HBM3 · 5.3TB/s · 84 TFLOPS FP64 · 1.3 PF BF16 · Infinity Fabric v3
  • Largest VRAM at launch — drove early BioNeMo and protein LLM inference adoption
  • Competitive FP64 vs H200 — viable for molecular dynamics and structural biology HPC
  • Open ROCm/HIP stack — attractive for academic medical centres and open-source bioinformatics
  • Deployed at scale by major hyperscalers — production-proven for cloud-based pharma pipelines
Verdict: Credible H200 alternative. Best AMD case is large-batch genomics inference (VRAM headroom) and open-stack GxP model validation environments.
MI350X
288GB HBM3e · ~8TB/s · ~108 TFLOPS FP64 est. · ~1.8 PF BF16 est.
  • 288GB matches B300/GB200 — AMD's direct Blackwell memory response for large MD systems
  • ~1.6× MI300X bandwidth uplift — strong for large-scale GROMACS and Cryo-EM pipelines
  • FP8 support expected — imaging AI and clinical NLP inference competitiveness improves
Verdict: Compelling for multi-vendor diversification. Especially relevant where open-source ROCm bioinformatics stacks are preferred. Pre-GA.
MI355X
288GB HBM3e · ~8TB/s · ~116 TFLOPS FP64 est. · ~2.0 PF BF16 est.
  • AMD flagship — ~2 PF BF16 approaches B200 inference territory for clinical NLP workloads
  • Optimised inference efficiency at lower TDP — favourable for green data centre mandates
  • Infinity Fabric v4 improvements address multi-GPU scale-out for multi-omics training
Verdict: AMD's top life science GPU. Best for vendor-diverse estates, academic HPC, or non-NVIDIA inference at scale. Pre-GA estimates.

  • 96K context handles most individual CSRs and ICH guideline documents

  • ~115 tok/s single-user decode — real-time investigator-facing regulatory Q&A is viable

  • 1M+ token context enables full-dossier regulatory corpus analysis — entire NDA/MAA as a single prompt

  • NVL72 unified memory eliminates KV cache spill — latency fully deterministic for real-time clinical AI

▌ AMD — Tokenization Highlights
MI300X
192GB HBM3 · Partial FP8 · ~22K prefill tok/s · 160K ctx

  • Competitive prefill vs H200 — strong for document-heavy batch clinical NLP workloads
  • ROCm/vLLM stack matures rapidly — viable for open-source pharmacovigilance and regulatory toolchains

MI355X
288GB HBM3e · FP8 support · ~46K prefill tok/s est. · 280K ctx est.

  • FP8 throughput estimated at ~1.8×: approaches B200 efficiency for clinical inference workloads
  • Lower TDP profile — favourable for energy-cost-sensitive hospital data centres and green pharma mandates

KV cache slot estimates assume 128K context window with KV cache in BF16 at 70B parameter model (approx. 16GB per 128K slot at BF16). Max context estimates assume full KV in-VRAM without offloading. TTFT estimates for 4K token prompt on a single GPU without tensor parallelism. ⚠ B300, GB300, MI350X, MI355X tokenization figures are architectural projections — not independently validated. FP8 throughput multipliers are indicative; real-world uplift depends on model architecture, quantisation quality, and serving framework maturity (TRT-LLM vs vLLM vs ROCm). AMD FP8 support on MI300X is partial via ROCm; MI350X/MI355X FP8 support assumed full but unconfirmed.
